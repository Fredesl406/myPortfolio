Overview

As part of a mechatronics course, the goal of this project was to explore the self-driving and intercommunication capabilities of robots using various sensors in a simulation environment. In five different scenarios, two or more robots had to demonstrate safe autonomous driving through a T-intersection crossing. The choice of sensors used for this task was ours to decide.

Implementation

To perform this task, robots were equipped with GPS, gyroscope and LiDAR sensors. Python scripts were developed for object detection, collision avoidance, lane-keeping and turning abilities of the robots. GPS was used to track precise coordinate points at which robots must begin turning. The gyroscope measured the orientation of the vehicle in the lane-keeping phase. Finally, LiDAR was used to map the surrounding environment, detect nearby vehicles and trigger the object avoidance function.

Result

Successfully implemented five scenarios (as seen above) where robots used vehicle-to-vehicle communication to avoid collisions without stopping at the intersection. The vehicle communication mimics behaviour found in nature, for example in ant colonies. A grade of 95% was obtained for the submission of this project.
