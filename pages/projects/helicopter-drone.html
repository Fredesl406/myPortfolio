Capstone Project: Helicopter Drone Design

September 2021 - December 2021

Overview

Our team of five was tasked with the design of a helicopter drone capable of meeting our client’s standards and requirements. The drone had to be portable with a removable tail boom, an independent tail pitch control and collapsible landing gear. Additionally, the main drive motor and rotor shaft had to be in an inline configuration. Finally, the battery had to be easily repositioned to compensate for the change in the center of mass from different payload configurations. The drone was designed to carry a maximum payload weight of 6kg and have an operating temperature range of -40°C to 50°C. It’s able to withstand 10m/s winds and stay in flight for 30 minutes at a time. In the event that the design criteria change, the CAD dimensions were made parametrizable through a simple user interface.

Implementation

The drone was designed by modelling and sourcing components in SolidWorks, using machine element design knowledge to model and mount shafts, gears and bearings, selecting suitable materials for each component and applying design for manufacturing and assembly (DFMA) principles throughout the computer-aided design process. Feasibility of design was ensured through extensive calculations and analysis of forces, torques and fatigue, statics and dynamics, kinematics, fluid mechanics, thermodynamics, energy consumption and by applying logical safety factors. Innovative solutions were developed to enhance drone portability by making the tail and legs detachable through a set of telescopic pins and allowing linear slideout of the tail shaft from the main gear hub using a male-female spline configuration. Design solutions were conceptualized through 10+ hand sketches before proceeding to CAD modelling. The drone model was parameterized in MATLAB for varying payload weight, size and survivable impact height in order to make the design more flexible to changing requirements. Given these inputs, the code calculates optimal dimensions of components and automatically updates the SolidWorks global variables to reflect the ideal model. 6 technical reports were prepared with the use of LaTeX and a formal presentation was given to the professor and client.

Result

All design criteria and requirements were met. Moreover, all deliverables were submitted within the tight deadlines that were given. A low-cost, simple and innovative approach was achieved to address portability and center of mass requirements.
