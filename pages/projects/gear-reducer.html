Design of an Anchor Windlass Gear Reducer

July 2020

Overview

The objective of this project was to design a gear reducer to be used as an anchor windlass in the boating industry. Some of the requirements included that the input and output shafts be perpendicular (90Â° turn) with an input power of 5 hp as well as an input and output velocity of 2000 rpm and 300 rpm respectively.

Implementation

To determine the most suitable gearbox configuration, the following criteria were evaluated in a decision matrix: compactness, noise, weight, cost, efficiency, durability and maintenance. A two-stage spherical-bevel and helical gearbox was identified as the best option. Design feasibility was then ensured through extensive calculations and analysis of gear forces and torques, bending fatigue, gear ratios and safety factors. Finally, the gearbox casing and shafts were modelled and suitable gears and bearings were sourced in SolidWorks. Machine element design fundamentals were used to model and mount shafts, gears and bearings following design for assembly (DFA) principles. The analysis was presented in a 50-page technical report.

Result

All design criteria and requirements were met. Sufficient calculations were performed to ensure the gearbox will be able to withstand the internal and external forces required for a long lifespan.
