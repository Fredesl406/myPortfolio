Design of a Portable Ziplining Device

January 2019 - April 2019

Overview

The objective of this project was to design a radically different and innovative portable ziplining device that does not require any preinstalled equipment in a forest. Moreover, it had to be eco-friendly, which means that it cannot produce combustion gases and must minimize damage to the surrounding trees. An emphasis was placed on the safety of the device and on reducing its weight.

Implementation

I led the development of initial concepts by brainstorming the overall design and functionality of the device, making detailed 2D & 3D hand sketches and using weighted decision matrices to evaluate design ideas. I designed the casing, zipline launching barrels and triggers using SolidWorks while sourcing a number of complex components. Our team managed time effectively by developing a Gantt chart to plan objectives and deadlines, coordinating design elements, and organizing team design reviews to discuss ideas and challenges. The final design consists of two launching barrels connected to a CO2 tank. Triggers on the side of the device are pressed to release pressure valves and launch a pair of grappling hooks which wrap around a tree trunk. Another hook is placed on the current tree and a motor is used to reel in the front spool cable while unwinding the back and hence enabling travel from tree to tree.

Result

The zipline device met all design criteria and requirements, as the motor is electric and the grappling hooks are not expected to cause too much damage to the trees. Conceptually, the device is simple and innovative. An A+ final grade was obtained on the project.
